# Lab 9: Sorting
TO: The 222 Sorting Consultants, Inc.

FROM: Unhappy Library, Ltd.

Mr. Gideon Gael, Circulation Manager

RE: Sorting Issues (Lab 9)

Dear 222 Sorting Consultants:

Due to our library’s hapless and frantic patrons wishing to check out all their books, we at Unhappy Library, Ltd. are struggling with the issue of sorting many books that are now out of order. Our collection is spread across many rooms with varying visibility and space accommodations, and some sections of books require special attention when putting back in order. When we heard you may be able to provide direction on how to sort in these different situations, we thought we’d reach out.

The following rooms/shelves in our library need sorting:

* A room of novels sorted by author’s last name that is completely reversed (is now Z-A), and needs to be resorted to run A-Z.
* A very dark room of obscure oversize history books. The room has an irreplaceable lightbulb illuminating the start of the shelf, and otherwise only a portable lamp with an astonishingly small radius, so you can only see your current book, and the very next book on the shelf. When you move to the next book, you cannot see the one you were previously looking at.
* A well-lit shelf of a small number of children’s high fantasy picture books that must be organized.
* A very cramped room with every science fiction novel ever written that needs to be sorted. The room is so cramped that there is no possibility of removing more than one book at a time.
* A disorganized but slightly-sorted collection of microfiches of newspapers detailing all elections in the 1890s, that, once sorted, must be kept in their relative order as given.

Please respond with the following details for each situation:

* Which sorting algorithm(s) would be most useful for each situation, and why.
* List some algorithms that will definitely not work for the given situation, and why.

Your response should be in a formal letter format, submitted by 1:20pm on Tuesday, to Canvas so we can discuss it at our next board meeting. The letter must be in the form of a **shared Google Doc link with editor access enabled**.

The reward for satisfactorily providing answers for all 5 situations is 2 points each, for a total of 10 points. We appreciate your assistance.

Sincerely,

Mr. Gideon Gael Circulation Manager, Unhappy Library, Ltd.
